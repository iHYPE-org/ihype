The recommendation engine · transparent by design

Discovery by place and momentum.

The algorithm reads three signals: what's hyped near you, what your friends love, and what's heating up across genres you've earned in. Every recommendation shows its math. Nothing pays for placement.

01 · How it works

Three inputs. One engine. Every output explainable.

A simple weighted blend you can audit. No black box. No paid boost. Geographic relevance, social proof, and HYPE momentum — that's the whole formula.

Inputs

What we read

  • Your county & region
  • Songs you've finished
  • Friends & their HYPE
  • Active HYPE on entities
  • Current geographic velocity
→
Engine

The blend

  • 40% taste match
  • 25% geographic weight
  • 20% social proof
  • 15% momentum velocity
  • 0% paid placement
→
Outputs

What you see

  • Ranked artist feed
  • Show recommendations
  • Tour-friendly venues (artists)
  • Booking radar (venues)
  • Audience matches (promoters)
Auditable

Every recommendation exposes its weight breakdown. Click the "why this?" panel to see the four signals at the score they hit.

Anti-monoculture

The cascade prevents global hits from drowning local scenes. Top three slots are reserved for sub-1,000-HYPE entities at fan request.

Unmonetized

No artist, label, or promoter can pay to rank higher. Sponsored slots appear at position 4+ and are labelled. Always.

02 · Geographic cascade

Local first. Global last.

Every entity in a recommendation is weighted by how close it is to you. The closer the source, the heavier the count. This is what keeps your feed from being national algorithm soup.

×1.0

04 · Transparency by default

Every recommendation shows its math.

If you don't know why something was recommended, you can't tell if you've been manipulated. iHYPE shows the four signals that drove every ranking — and never hides paid placement.

04
Signal weights are public.
Taste 40%, geo 25%, social 20%, momentum 15%. Posted in the docs, with a changelog every time the board votes a change.
$0
Sponsored slots are labelled.
When a music ad reaches a recommendation surface, it's tagged "Sponsored," appears at position 4+, and never displaces top organic results.
∞
You can audit anyone.
Tap "why this?" on any recommendation. See the breakdown. Disagree? Flag it — flags route to public moderation review.
