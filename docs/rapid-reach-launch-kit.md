# iHYPE Rapid-Reach Launch Kit

Prepared for the Maine founding-scene pilot.

## Launch objective

Concentrate enough activity in one market to prove the discovery-to-booking loop:

- 25 founding artists
- 5 founding venues
- 10 DJs/promoters
- 5 distribution partners
- 250 listener registrations
- 100 showcase RSVPs
- 3 documented venue booking-intent conversations
- 1 paid booking influenced by iHYPE

The goal is useful density, not nationwide emptiness with impressive impression counts.

## Seven-day activation

### Day 1: Cohort recruitment

- Publish `/launch`.
- Invite 25 artists, 5 venues, 10 DJs/promoters, and 5 partners directly.
- Track every invitation in the prospect sheet.
- Ask each invitee for one explicit commitment and one introduction.

### Day 2: Roster and content intake

Collect from each artist:

- artist name, city, genre, short bio
- profile image and approved media
- one featured track or Seed
- upcoming dates and booking availability
- social handles and email-list size range
- preferred launch clip or timestamp

Collect from venues:

- capacity and neighborhood
- booking contact
- preferred genres and event formats
- available showcase dates
- newsletter, calendar, and social reach ranges

### Day 3: Share-kit production

Produce for every participant:

- one vertical clip
- one square image
- one short caption
- one longer email/newsletter paragraph
- one tracked registration or artist link
- one coordinated publishing time

### Day 4: Partner activation

Ask each partner for one concrete distribution action:

- newsletter placement
- social post
- calendar listing
- radio interview or airplay segment
- artist introductions
- showcase space
- sponsorship or in-kind production support

### Day 5: Showcase announcement

- Publish artist lineup and venue partner.
- Open RSVPs and reminders.
- Release the first artist spotlight.
- Confirm streaming, moderation, rights, and backup procedures.

### Day 6: Coordinated release

- Publish all artist, venue, DJ, and partner posts in a two-hour window.
- Repost every participant through iHYPE.
- Run direct-message follow-up with people who engage.
- Capture referrals with tracked links.

### Day 7: Publish proof

- Release a scene chart based on transparent activity.
- Publish clips and preliminary results.
- Share booking-interest conversations without overstating outcomes.
- Announce the next showcase before attention decays into the usual digital fog.

## Resource activation checklist

### Immediate

- Publish the founding-scene page.
- Recruit the first cohort through direct invitations.
- Choose one showcase date and venue.
- Prepare shared clips, images, captions, and tracked links.
- Create one press release and one media list.
- Track all outreach and follow-up dates.

### Nonprofit technology

- Apply for Google for Nonprofits and Google Ad Grants after eligibility is documented.
- Apply for Canva Nonprofits after charitable registration or eligible sponsorship documentation is ready.
- Register with TechSoup and review available nonprofit technology offers.
- Configure analytics around registrations, role, referral source, RSVPs, HYPE activity, and booking intent.

### Funding

- Prepare the Maine Expansion Arts application before the July 15 opening.
- Confirm that the mission and entity/fiscal-sponsor arrangement meet program eligibility.
- Request local support letters and in-kind commitments.
- Build a one-page founding donor brief tied to measurable pilot outcomes.

## Measurement

Track daily:

- invitations sent by audience type
- responses and commitments
- profiles completed
- referral-link visits
- registrations by role and source
- artist media published
- RSVPs and reminder opt-ins
- show attendance and watch time
- HYPE actions and unique participants
- venue booking-interest actions
- bookings influenced and artist compensation

Report weekly:

- cohort conversion by source
- cost per registration
- share-to-registration conversion
- RSVP-to-attendance conversion
- participant retention
- venue follow-up and booking pipeline
- partner distribution contributed

## Guardrails

- Do not use pay-to-rank placement.
- Clearly label sponsorships and promoted content.
- Do not sell listener identities or behavioral profiles.
- Require artists to control removal of future media use.
- Exclude removed media from future promoter shows while preserving only the rights already granted for previously created shows.
- Keep advertising music-related and mission-aligned.
- Verify rights, takedown, refund, privacy, moderation, and charitable-solicitation requirements before expanding public activity.
