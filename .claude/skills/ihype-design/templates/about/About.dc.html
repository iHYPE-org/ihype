Not-for-profit · Built for the scene

About iHYPE

iHYPE exists to give independent artists, venues, and promoters a platform that doesn't take a cut. No platform fee on tickets. No streaming royalty deductions. No algorithmic pay-to-play. Built and governed by the community it serves.

Our promise

What we will never do

◐

0% ticket fee

Face value is face value. Every cent from a ticket sale goes directly to the artist or venue — no platform deduction, ever.

♡

No streaming cuts

iHYPE does not take royalties. Hypes are a demand signal, not a revenue share mechanism.

◇

No ads

The platform is funded by the community, not by advertisers. Your listening habits are never sold.

◉

Open to all

Any independent artist, venue, or promoter can join for free. There is no gated tier, no premium listing, no pay-to-surface.

Funding

How it's funded

iHYPE is funded entirely by advertising, the same way terrestrial radio has always worked — and those ads are restricted to music-related sources only, forever. No user-data resale funds this platform, and no ticket revenue is ever touched.

Our story

How we got here

January 2026

Founded in Portland, ME

iHYPE incorporated as a not-for-profit organization in Portland, Maine. The founding charter locked in 0% platform fees and the 70/20/10 revenue split.

February 2026

Design system & charter published

The iHYPE design system and community charter were published openly. The revenue split, governance model, and founding principles are locked in the charter.

March 2026

Platform beta launched

Beta access opened to artists, venues, and promoters in the Portland area. First shows went live on the platform.

June 2026

Expanded beta & radio shows

Fan beta access opened nationally. Audio-only radio shows and live DJ sets went live. iHYPE has never hosted video and never will.

The team

Who builds iHYPE

iHYPE is built by a small team of musicians, developers, and community organizers who got tired of watching independent venues close and independent artists lose income to platform fees. We are not venture-backed. We do not have a growth-at-all-costs mandate.
