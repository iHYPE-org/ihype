How it works

Discover, hype, buy direct.

Every step of the iHYPE experience is built to move value from the middlemen back to the artists and the fans who found them first.

01 · HYPE

02 · TICKETS

FAQ

Common questions.

What is the 70/20/10 split?

Every ticket sold on iHYPE is split: 70% to the artist, 20% to the venue (configurable), and up to 10% to the promoter pool — paid to whoever's referral link drove the purchase. iHYPE takes 0% — operations are funded by the nonprofit, not the gate. The only charge above face value is the card-processing fee, passed through at cost (Stripe: 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction; AMEX 3.5% + $0.30) — iHYPE takes $0. The split is frozen at event publish — it cannot change.

How do I earn as a promoter?

Any fan or DJ can grab a referral link for any event. When someone buys through your link, you earn min(10%, 10% × gross-you-drove ÷ event-total). Drive the whole gate, earn the full 10%. Drive a sliver, earn proportionally. Payouts settle the night of the show.

What's the free-use library for DJs?

Artists can upload tracks under a limited free-use license. DJs can crate only those tracks and use them in radio shows — voice + SFX + music, all royalty-clear. Listeners discover the artists; artists gain exposure. It's opt-in for artists and gated for DJs at the platform level.

Is iHYPE a real nonprofit?

Our 501(c) application is pending. We are incorporated as a nonprofit and operate under those principles — no private equity, no ads, no selling fan data. Banking and real payouts go live once the filing clears. Beta payments are simulated.

What platforms is iHYPE on?

The beta runs on desktop web, mobile web, iOS, and Android — all from a single codebase. Native app store submissions are planned after the closed beta closes.
